What is a black hole, and how do they form?

A black hole is a region in space where the gravitational
pull is so intense that nothing, not even light, can escape
its grasp. They form when massive stars undergo
supernova explosions, causing their cores to collapse
under gravitational forces. If the core's mass exceeds a
critical threshold, it contracts infinitely, forming a
singularity at the center surrounded by an event horizon
â€“ a boundary beyond which nothing can return. Black
holes come in various sizes, from stellar-mass ones to
supermassive ones found at the centers of galaxies.
They are fascinating objects that challenge our
understanding of space, time, and gravity.
